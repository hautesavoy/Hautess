The style evolution of the Migos

Oftentimes the development of an artist is exhibited through their music, and perceived popularity. However, in the case of the Migo’s, along with other rap artists (e.g., A$AP Rocky, and Kanye West) fashion provides an additional outlet through which their development, and self-expression are displayed.


"Versace, Versace, Medusa head on me like I'm 'Luminati"- Quavo

The Migos have been prominent figures within their respective industry for quiet a while. Producing countless hits such as Versace, Fight Night, Pipe It Up, and most recently Bad and Boujee. While their addictive and cohesive rhyme schemes have arguably remained unchanged, the Migos have never shied away from adapting their style and fashion choices.

Tasteful, yet ostentatious, the fashion evolution of the Migos has caught up to their equally extravagant sound. Whether at the club—donning rock inspired T-shirts with the jackets to match— or at the Met Gala in a Versace ensemble, the fashion sense of the Migos has certainly matched their raise to fame, all whilst remaining distinctive in character.
